Methodology

1. Regime Classification Framework

The regime detection system combines policy-based and market-based indicators using dynamic inverse-variance weighting to identify four distinct market regimes.

Primary Score (60% Weight): Policy-Based Indicators

Indicator Weight Rationale
Economic Policy Uncertainty (EPU) 60% Captures uncertainty around fiscal, regulatory, and monetary policy
Geopolitical Risk (GPR) 15% Measures geopolitical tensions and war risk
Inflation YoY 15% Tracks inflationary pressures affecting fixed income
Government Spending/GDP 10% Indicates fiscal expansion or contraction

Secondary Score (40% Weight): Market-Based Indicators

  • VIX (Volatility Index): Market fear gauge
  • Investment Grade Spreads: Credit market stress
  • High Yield Spreads: Risk appetite indicator
  • Unemployment Rate: Economic health measure
  • Yield Curve Spread (10Y-2Y): Recession predictor
  • 10Y Treasury Yield Change: Interest rate dynamics

Dynamic Weighting & Signal Processing

  • Inverse-Variance Weighting: Automatically adjusts weights based on 36-month rolling variance
  • Z-Score Normalization: 60-month lookback window
  • Percentile Calculation: 180-month rolling window
  • Smoothing: Minimum regime duration of 6 months, max 3-month gap filling

2.2 Tactical Tilts

Continuous, signal-driven adjustments applied on top of baseline allocations:

Tilt Type Trigger Condition Action Max Impact
Inflation Tilt Inflation z-score > 1.0 +TIPS, -HY, -IG ±2-3pp
Government Spending Tilt Gov spending z-score > 0.8 +Government, -HY ±1.5pp
Combined Score Tilt Combined score > 1.2 +Defensive, -Risk ±1pp
VIX Tail Hedge VIX > 25 +Gold, +FX (CHF/JPY) ±3pp each

Tilt Constraints

  • Maximum single tilt: 15 percentage points
  • Total maximum tilt: 15 percentage points
  • Gold boost cap: 3pp
  • FX boost cap: 3pp

2.3 Rebalancing & Smoothing

  • Rebalancing Frequency: Quarterly
  • Smoothing Window: 3-month moving average
  • Covariance Estimation: 20% shrinkage (Ledoit-Wolf style)

Multi-layer risk control system applied sequentially to final allocations:

Layer 1: Volatility Targeting

  • Target: 3% annualized volatility
  • Buffer: 20% tolerance (trigger at 3.6%)
  • Action: If realized vol > 3.6%, shift allocation toward cash/short-term treasuries

Layer 2: Drawdown Defense

  • Trigger: -5% drawdown threshold
  • Action: Increase cash by up to 15%, proportionally reduce risky assets
  • Recovery: Gradual reversion as drawdown improves

Layer 3: Tail Hedging

  • Trigger: VIX > 30 OR combined score in top 10%
  • Action: Allocate to crisis hedges (Gold, safe-haven currencies CHF/JPY)
  • Magnitude: Scaled by severity of signal

Layer 4: Drawdown Budget

  • Monitor: Cumulative drawdown over rolling periods
  • Action: Reduce risk exposure proportionally to drawdown depth
  • Purpose: Prevent cascade losses during prolonged downturns

Methodology References

  • Regime Detection: Dynamic inverse-variance weighting of policy and market indicators
  • Allocation Framework: Regime-specific baselines with tactical tilts
  • Risk Management: 4-layer system (vol targeting, DD defense, tail hedging, DD budget)
  • Rebalancing: Quarterly with 3-month smoothing
  • Covariance Estimation: 20% Ledoit-Wolf shrinkage

Key Assumptions

  • No transaction costs or taxes included
  • Perfect liquidity assumed for all assets
  • Quarterly rebalancing feasible without market impact
  • Indicator data available in real-time (no lag)
  • TIPS data backfilled using available history (introduced mid-1990s)
  • Safe-haven FX (CHF/JPY) limited to recent data (2.1% coverage)
